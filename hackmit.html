Massachusetts Institute of Technology's HackMIT 2018

  • Sept 15 - 16, 2018
  • In Hackathons Life Experiences

This article is meant for sharing my experiences and not for any technical discussions. I do not intend to offend any person/institute or authority. In case you feel that I've written or mentioned something objectionable, please feel free to contact me on mrbharatmehra@gmail.com

Background Story

I am a student pursuing a B.Tech in Computer Science at VIT Vellore but my true passion lies in understanding how users interact with an application. Before coming to VIT, I had learnt how to make movies, trailers and posters for my school (Modern School, Barakhamba Road, New Delhi) events and club promotions. I learnt Adobe Photoshop, After Effects, Premiere Pro, Sony Vegas and 3Ds Max. However, most of my work was limited to making Event Posters and Event Trailers. I did not learnt these for my resume, I learnt design and filming because I loved the way it connected with people. I wanted to learn how to captivate an audience, how to make an audience fall in love with your content.


(LEFT: Me and my best friends standing in front of my poster | RIGHT: The last poster I made for Modern School's Tech Symposium ACCESS)
Finding my Sweet Spot

After entering an engineering college, I realised that the same design softwares are being used to make User Interfaces and Prototypes for Apps. Until now, I thought that whatever softwares I learnt in school would be useless in a tech environment where coding reigns and programming is the only skill you can have. I was wrong. Turns out that every single application did need design and prototyping. I instantly started practising Ul/UX and Prototyping and began to compete at technical events and Hackathons conducted at VIT. I was never alone, I had a strong team of Developers, Programmers and Machine Learning experts. It was a major shift from making Event Posters to making User Interfaces. I had to understand how all domains work (i0S, Android, Web) because I had to understand what could be possible to design. I was soon becoming good at behaving like a bridge between a technical coder and a non-technical user.

I had finally recognised a sweet spot where I could use my experience at Design and Marketing and use it for development and technology.
